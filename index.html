Cricieth, Gales

artist

Steffan Warren, chief editor

Kseniya Glagoleva, project manager

The medieval ruins of Cricieth Castle overlook the town from a rocky outcrop overlooking the sea. It is believed to have been built by Llewelyn the Great in the 13th century. Eight hundred years later, the self-proclaimed Pearl of Wales on the shores of Snowdonia has become a popular tourist destination during the summer months.

Just a short walk from the castle, you can enjoy the world's best ice cream at Cadwalader's, whose secret ingredient is rumored to be local seaweed. Cricieth is also famous for winning the Wales in Bloom award five years in a row for its spectacular floral displays around the town. It was also the birthplace of David Lloyd George, the only Welshman to have served as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom.

Berea, EE. UU.

artist

Travis Turner, author and editor

Berea is a small town located in central Kentucky. The town is surrounded by beautiful forests and fields. Known as the craft capital of the state, visitors will find endless shopping opportunities: jewelry stores, candle shops, and handcrafted woodwork; galleries, glass workshops, and more. The town holds an annual festival that pays tribute to "spoonbread," a local dish made from cornbread and served with a wooden spoon.

Although, it's probably best known for its university. Berea College was founded in 1855 and was the first racially integrated southern university, as well as the first to be coeducational. Something that somewhat makes it unique is that it doesn't charge tuition: each student receives a 100% scholarship.

Muramvya, Burundi

artist

Grevisse Kenguruka, technical editor

Muramvya is one of Burundi's 18 provinces. During the reign of the kingdom, Muramvya was its capital; and in 2017, thanks to its cultural and natural landscape, it was added to the UNESCO Tentative List of World Heritage Sites. It is located in central Burundi, between the country's political and economic capitals.

Its climate, but during the day, you might think you're in paradise. At 2,665 meters (8,743 feet) above sea level, Mount Teza is one of the coldest places in the province. But it's precisely that cool breeze that gives rise to one of the largest tea and coffee plantations in the country, which accounts for the majority of Burundi's exports.

Kibira National Park, one of the largest wildlife reserves for apes, occupies parts of four provinces, including Muramvya. This national park is located at the peak of the beautiful Congo-Nile Divide Mountains, whose heights range between 1,550 and 2,660 meters. It is full of beautiful vegetation and is a source for the various rivers and streams that provide water throughout the country.

Mexico City

artist

Francisco Sanchez, project manager

Mexico City is a vibrant city steeped in history. Originally founded by the Aztecs in 1325 as Tenochtitlan, this city has witnessed a rich cultural and architectural evolution.

The city is home to an impressive architectural diversity, with styles ranging from Gothic to contemporary, including archaeological remains and buildings of great historical value.

Mexico City is also an incomparable cultural destination, with more than 190 museums, second only to London in the number of museum institutions. Its rich history is reflected in World Heritage sites such as the Historic Center, Xochimilco, and the UNAM University Campus. Furthermore, in 1968, it hosted the Olympic Games, making it a sporting and cultural landmark in Latin America.
